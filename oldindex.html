Michael R Sweet

I'm a computer scientist who happens to love computers. I currently work for Apple Inc., am the chair of the Printer Working Group, and previously ran Easy Software Products with my wife (Tammy). When I am not writing software, standards, or books, I spend time with my wife and son and take lots of photos.

I am probably best known as the creator of CUPS, the de-facto standard printing system for Linux and UNIX, and for my work on the Internet Printing Protocol (IPP). I also wrote most of the CUPS documentation and am the author of the CUPS book - no, I didn't pick the picture for the cover:

In a former life I did a lot of IRIS GL and OpenGL computer graphics work and co-wrote the first two editions of the OpenGL SuperBible with Richard S. Wright, Jr. - the second edition features an image generated by one of my examples in the book:
